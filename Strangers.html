Game Development

Location: online, Dieburg
Role: Game Developer, Social media Campaign

As an indie game fan, I believe in the transformative power of interactive media to foster understanding and inspire change.
Passionate about media and net art, this project reflects my commitment to blending gaming with meaningful narratives to highlight and address the multifaceted challenges faced by women online.

"Stranger" is a role-playing, retro-style game developed for my Master's thesis, exploring the nuanced experiences of women in the digital age. The project examines the impact of the Internet on women through net art, highlighting specific types of crime in online spaces and their dual role in promoting and challenging feminist issues. Utilizing the Twine Engine, "Stranger" engages players in critical issues such as online crime and misogyny, offering an experience that balances enlightenment with entertainment.

The project has exhibited at the Schloss Fechenbach Museum.


On Social Media

The social media campaign for this project draws inspiration from 90s net art projects and successful Instagram campaigns. I have utilized Instagram's tagging function to mimic the experience of clicking a link on a website.

Each post is designed to function as if it were opening files or folders, or visiting a website, and each is connected to another account. This innovative approach is inspired by the Ebay Korea Escape Room project on Instagram.
